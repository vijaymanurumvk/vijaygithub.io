Abstract

Soil pollution is one of the most pressing environmental challenges of our time. This project explores the causes, current control mechanisms, and offers a proposal for reducing soil pollution effectively.

Existing System

The current methods of soil pollution control include limited regulations, waste management practices, and agricultural guidelines that lack efficiency in curbing pollution at a larger scale.

Proposed System

This project proposes stricter waste management policies, industrial soil quality checks, and the implementation of organic farming practices to reduce contamination and rehabilitate polluted lands.

Advantages

  • Improved soil quality.
  • Enhanced agricultural yield.
  • Reduction in health hazards for nearby populations.
  • Long-term environmental sustainability.

Disadvantages

  • High initial implementation cost.
  • Requires continuous monitoring and regulation.
  • Potential resistance from industries and farmers.

Conclusion

While addressing soil pollution may require significant effort and investment, the long-term benefits of cleaner, healthier soil ecosystems far outweigh the disadvantages. Collaboration across sectors is crucial for the success of this initiative.
